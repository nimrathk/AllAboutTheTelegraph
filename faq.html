FAQs: Telegraph!

Frequently asked questions!




What were some drawbacks of the Telegraph?

One major drawback of the Telegraph was having knowledge of Morse code. To send and receive messages operators needed to memorize Morse code, which was a series of short and long clicks. These clicks symbolized the dots and dashes which were representations of the alphabet.


Was there a word limit applied to messages sent via the Telegraph?

Yes, telegraph users were restricted by word limit. During the late 19th-century telegraph boom, some carriers charged extra for words longer than 15 characters and for messages longer than 10 words. Thus, the cheapest telegram was often limited to 150 characters.


How much did it cost to send a message via the telegraph in or around the period of the American Civil War?

Around the 1860s, a ten-word telegram sent from New York to New Orleans cost $2.70 which is about $95 today. When the transcontinental telegraph opened, the cost was $7.40 for ten words, which would equal $134 today. Over a period of time, prices decreased.
